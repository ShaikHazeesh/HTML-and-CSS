Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Remembering the People's President

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the "Missile Man of India," inspired millions with his wisdom, humility, and contributions to science. His life and teachings continue to guide us towards a better future.

Highlights of Dr. Kalam's Life

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram

    Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born in a small village of Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, on October 15, 1931.

  • 1969: Joined ISRO

    He joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was a project director of India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).

  • 1998: Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests

    He played a pivotal role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998, establishing India as a nuclear power.

  • 2002: Became President of India

    Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, winning hearts across the country.

  • 2015: Passed Away

    On July 27, 2015, Dr. Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, doing what he loved — inspiring others.
